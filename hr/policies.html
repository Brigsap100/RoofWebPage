Company Policies & Handbook

Key sections of the Kodiak Employee Handbook. Expand any section for a quick summary — the full signed PDFs live on the company drive.

01

Code of Conduct

Every Kodiak employee is expected to act with honesty, respect, and professionalism on jobsites, in the office, and with clients. This means treating coworkers, customers, and the public courteously, protecting company and client property, and avoiding conflicts of interest. Drug- and alcohol-free workplace rules apply to all personnel, and reporting to work impaired is grounds for termination.

Confidential business information — pricing, bids, client records, and CRM data — must be kept secure and never shared outside the company. Violations of the code should be reported to a supervisor or HR; Kodiak prohibits retaliation against anyone who reports a concern in good faith.

02

Safety Program (IIPP)

Kodiak maintains a written Injury and Illness Prevention Program (IIPP) as required by Cal/OSHA. The program assigns safety responsibility at every level, requires regular jobsite inspections, and documents hazard identification and correction. Our Safety Officer coordinates training, and every crew holds a documented toolbox talk before work begins each day.

Fall protection is the cornerstone of roofing safety: guardrails, personal fall-arrest systems, or warning-line systems are required whenever crews work at height per the fall-protection plan. All injuries and near-misses must be reported to your foreman immediately so they can be logged, investigated, and corrected. Repeated safety violations lead to progressive discipline up to termination.

03

Jobsite Procedures

Each project starts with a site-specific safety plan and a hazard walk led by the foreman. Crews stage materials to keep egress paths and fire lanes clear, secure loose materials against wind, and set up hot-work and torch-down zones with a charged extinguisher and a fire-watch when required. Ladders and hoists are inspected before use and tied off or secured.

Housekeeping is continuous: debris is cleared to designated dumpsters, fasteners and cutoffs are picked up throughout the day, and the site is left broom-clean at shutdown. Crews are responsible for protecting the client's property — landscaping, HVAC units, and finished surfaces — and for respectful, professional conduct while on a customer's premises.

04

Harassment & Anti-Discrimination Policy

Kodiak is committed to a workplace free of harassment and discrimination. We do not tolerate harassment based on race, color, religion, sex, gender identity, sexual orientation, national origin, age, disability, veteran status, or any other protected characteristic. This applies to conduct on jobsites, in the office, at company events, and in electronic communications.

Employees who experience or witness harassment should report it to a supervisor, HR, or through any manager they are comfortable with — there is no single required channel. Every complaint is investigated promptly and as confidentially as possible. Retaliation against anyone who reports a concern or participates in an investigation is strictly prohibited and itself a violation of this policy.

05

PPE Requirements

Personal protective equipment is mandatory on all jobsites. At minimum, crews wear hard hats, ANSI-rated safety glasses, high-visibility shirts or vests, and slip-resistant work boots. Cut-resistant gloves, hearing protection, and respirators are required for specific tasks such as cutting, grinding, or working with solvents and coatings.

Fall-protection harnesses and lanyards must be inspected before each use and taken out of service if worn or damaged. Kodiak provides required PPE and an annual boot allowance; employees are responsible for wearing it correctly and keeping it in good condition. Working without required PPE is a serious safety violation.

06

Attendance & Punctuality

Reliable attendance keeps crews productive and safe. Report to your assigned jobsite or the yard ready to work at your scheduled start time. If you will be late or absent, notify your foreman as early as possible — at least one hour before your shift — so coverage can be arranged. Weather delays are communicated by your foreman or through the crew dispatch text.

Unexcused absences and repeated tardiness are addressed through progressive discipline. Time off should be requested through the PTO process in advance whenever possible. Three consecutive days of no-call/no-show are treated as job abandonment. Questions about your schedule or attendance record go to your foreman or HR.
